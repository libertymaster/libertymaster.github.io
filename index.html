What About Liberty?

What is liberty?

According to Merriam-Webster Dictionary:

lib·​er·​ty ˈli-bər-tē 1: the quality or state of being free: a: the power to do as one pleases b: freedom from physical restraint c: freedom from arbitrary or despotic (see despot sense 1) control d: the positive enjoyment of various social, political, or economic rights and privileges e: the power of choice

According to The American Heritage® Dictionary of the English Language, 5th Edition:

lib·er·ty (lĭbər-tē) 1. The condition of being free from confinement, servitude, or forced labor. 2. a. The condition of being free from oppressive restriction or control by a government or other power. b. A right to engage in certain actions without control or interference by a government or other power: the liberties protected by the Bill of Rights. 3. The right or power to act as one chooses.

According to the Oxford English Dictionary:

lib·er·ty I. The state or condition of being free. I.1.a. Theology. Freedom from the bondage or dominating influence of sin, spiritual servitude, worldly ties, etc. I.1.b. Freedom or release from slavery, bondage, or imprisonment. I.1.c. Freedom from arbitrary, despotic, or autocratic control; independence, esp. from a foreign power, monarchy, or dictatorship. civil liberty n. I.2.a. The condition of being able to act or function without hindrance or restraint; faculty or power to do as one likes. See also personal liberty n. I.2.b. Philosophy and Theology. The fact of not being controlled by or subject to fate; freedom of will. Frequently opposed to necessity. liberty of will n. Freedom is now the more common term (see freedom n.). I.2.c. Chiefly in plural. Each of those social and political freedoms which are considered to be the entitlement of all members of a community; a civil liberty. liberty of speech n. liberty of conscience n. liberty of religion n.
